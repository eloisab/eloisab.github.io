Stirling Engine


This class served as an introduction to machining through the design and manufacture of a gamma-type Stirling Engine. We were responsible for the design of about half of the parts of our individual Stirling engines including the flywheel, connecting rods, bedplate, and base. We also machined of almost all of the parts including tooling. The parts were manufactured using manual lathes, manual mills, and ProtoTRAK CNC mills. In the end, my engine ran at 1162 rpm with a crème brulèe torch applied.

Flappy Bird

For my ENGR105 final project, I created a version of flappy bird in MATLAB. I focused on making a game that looked really similar to the original flappy bird. This wasn't particularly easy as MATLAB isn't really built for those sort of products. Collision detection worked from the transparency data of the image I used and the game is easily editable for different computer speeds. I was really pleased with the final game and have yet to score higher than five.


Parallel-Axis Gripper

Summer of 2014 I worked in Modlab. I spent the summer reverse engineering Willow Garage's Velo Gripper in an attempt to make a cheaper one for the lab to use. The project required figuring out the mechanics behind the Velo Gripper without taking it apart and improving them, Solidworks modeling of the parts, and 3D printing the models. It took most of the summer, but by the end the group I was working with had successfully developed a working parallel-axis gripper.
