Variable Pitch Quadcopter

Myself and two friends decided to spend spring break making a variable pitch quadcopter. We decided to make a variable pitch quadcopter for the added mechanical design challenge as well as cool abilities like being able to fly upsidedown. We designed and made the drivetrain and chassis. The chassis is lasercut. Drive shafts were machined. The propellors are spun using a single brushless motor and belts. Pitch actuation is controlled through four servos.


We created a MATLAB script to calculate the outer diameter of the belt using the center-to-center distance between the pulley pitch diameters we choose. Pulley pitch diameters were determined by using the kV rating of our motor and recommended RPM of the propellers.

We choose to use a belt drive because it's lightweight, easy to fix, and simple to design for.
