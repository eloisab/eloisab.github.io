Stirling Engine


This class served as an introduction to machining through the design and manufacture of a gamma-type Stirling Engine. We were responsible for the design of about half of the parts of our individual Stirling engines including the flywheel assembly, connecting rods, bedplate, and base. We also machined almost all of the parts, including tooling. The parts were manufactured using manual lathes, manual mills, and ProtoTRAK CNC mills. In the end, my engine ran at 1162 rpm with a crème brulèe torch applied.
